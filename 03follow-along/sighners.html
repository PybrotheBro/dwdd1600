Who where the signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration and later the third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned inventor, philosopher, and statesman, who was a key figure in securing French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence and the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A vocal leader of the American Revolution and cousin of John Adams, known for his role in organizing resistance to British rule.
John Hancock
The president of the Continental Congress, famous for his bold signature on the Declaration.
Patrick Henry
A passionate orator and leader, famous for his "Give me liberty, or give me death!" speech, though he did not sign the Declaration, he played an important role in the revolution.
Richard Henry Lee
Proposed the resolution for independence in June 1776, which led to the Declaration.

These individuals were pivotal in shaping the nation's early history and advocating for the colonies' independence from Britain.
